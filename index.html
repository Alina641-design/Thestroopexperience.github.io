The Stroop Experience

When visiting Amsterdam, there are a few things every traveler must try — and stroopwafels are at the top of that list.

These thin, caramel-filled waffle cookies have been a Dutch favorite since the late 18th century, when they were first created in the city of Gouda. Originally made from leftover crumbs sweetened with syrup, stroopwafels grew from a humble street snack into one of the most beloved treats in the Netherlands.

Today, no trip to Amsterdam is complete without experiencing this sweet tradition for yourself. But with so many shops claiming to make “the best stroopwafel,” where should you go? That’s where The Stroop Experience comes in.

Inside this gem of a bakery, the stroopwafel is still crafted daily by hand and the enduring combination of grandeur and artisanal baking makes Firma Stroop a beloved destination for locals and tourists alike. Many visitors pause, camera in hand, to capture the stunning exterior and interior before indulging in its warm, treacle‑flavored, syrupy treats. Please feel free to step in, even just for the pictures.

We guide you through three of the most popular stroopwafel spots in the city, so you can taste history, compare flavors, and decide which stroopwafel is truly your favorite.
